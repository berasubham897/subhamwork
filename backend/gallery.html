DARJEELING

  • Darjeeling is a town in India's West Bengal state, in the Himalayan foothills. Once a summer resort for the British Raj elite, it remains the terminus of the narrow-gauge Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, or “Toy Train,” completed in 1881. It's famed for the distinctive black tea grown on plantations that dot its surrounding slopes. Its backdrop is Mt. Kanchenjunga, among the world’s highest peaks.

  • Tiger Hill, Batasia Loop, Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park, Peace Pagoda, Ghum Monastery, Rock Garden
  • Mumbai (formerly called Bombay) is a densely populated city on India’s west coast. A financial center, it's India's largest city. On the Mumbai Harbour waterfront stands the iconic Gateway of India stone arch, built by the British Raj in 1924. Offshore, nearby Elephanta Island holds ancient cave temples dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva. The city's also famous as the heart of the Bollywood film industry.
  • 8 days & 7 night

  • Marine Drive, Bandra-Worli Sea Link, Gateway of India, Kanheri Caves, Global Vipassana Pagoda, Shree Siddhivinayak, Nariman Point, Elephanta Caves, Mani Bhaban Gandhi Museum

KOLKATA

  • Kolkata (formerly Calcutta) is the capital of India's West Bengal state. Founded as an East India Company trading post, it was India's capital under the British Raj from 1773–1911. Today it’s known for its grand colonial architecture, art galleries and cultural festivals. It’s also home to Mother House, headquarters of the Missionaries of Charity, founded by Mother Teresa, whose tomb is on site.

  • Victoria Memorial Hall, St. Paul Cathedral Church, Alipore ZOO, Nicco Park, Eco Park & MWM, Aquatica, Science City, Indian Museum

VIZAG

  • Visakhapatnam is a port city and industrial center in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh, on the Bay of Bengal. It's known for its many beaches, including Ramakrishna Beach, home to a preserved submarine at the Kursura Submarine Museum. Nearby are the elaborate Kali Temple and the Visakha Museum, an old Dutch bungalow housing local maritime and historical exhibits.
  • 5 days & 4 night
  • Only Rs. 5,999 /- (per head)
  • Yarada Beach, Katiki Waterfalls, Araku Valley, Kailasagiri, Borra Caves, Submarine Museum, Indira Gandhi Zoological Park
  • Amritsar is a city in the northwestern Indian state of Punjab, 28 kilometers from the border with Pakistan. At the center of its walled old town, the gilded Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib) is the holiest gurdwara (religious complex) of the Sikh religion. It’s at the end of a causeway, surrounded by the sacred Amrit Sarovar tank (lake), where pilgrims bathe.

  • Akal Takht, Wagah Border, Jallianwala Bagh, Gobindgarh Fort, Durgiana Temple, Partition Museum, Khairuddin Mosque, Thunder Zone Amusement And Water Park, The Golden Temple
